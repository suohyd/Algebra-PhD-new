Definition 1.6.1. Quaternion algebra.

Let \(a,b\in\dot{F}\text{.}\) The quaternion algebra \((a,b)_F\) or \(\left(\tfrac{a,b}{F}\right)\) is the four-dimensional \(F\)-vector space with basis \(\{1,i,j,k\}\) and multiplication defined by the following relations and with \(1\) as unity of \((a,b)_F\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} i^2 = a\cdot 1, \quad j^2 = b\cdot 1, \quad ij = k = -ji.\tag{1.6.1} \end{equation}
We assume the associativity of multiplication and extend the multiplication to all elements of \((a,b)_F\) by distributivity and linearity.
