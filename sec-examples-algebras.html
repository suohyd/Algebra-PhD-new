    Section 1.2 Some examples of algebras

    Example 1.2.1. Associative ring as \(\Z\)-algbera.

    Every associative ring \(A\) can be considered as a \(\Z\)-algebra.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.2.2. Ring as an algebra over itself.

    Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with unity. Then \(R\) can be considered a right \(R\)-module using the multiplication in ring \(R\text{.}\) With this considerations, \(R\) is an \(R\)-algebra.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.2.3. Ring over its center.

    Suppose that \(A\) is a ring (not necessarily commutative), and let \(Z(A)\) be the center of \(A\text{.}\) Then \(A\) is a \(Z(A)\)-algebra.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.2.4. Matrix algebra.

    Suppose that \(R\) is a commutative ring and \(n\) is a positive integer. Suppose that \(A\) is an \(R\)-algebra. Then the ring of \(n\times n\) matrices over \(A\text{,}\) denoted by \(M_n(A)\text{,}\) is an \(R\)-algebra.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.2.5. Endomorphism algebra.

    Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with unity. Suppose that \(M\) is a right \(R\)-module. The endomorphism ring \(\End{R}{M}\) is an \(R\)-algebra.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.2.6. Polynomial ring as algebra.

    Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with unity. The polynomial ring \(R[X]\) is an \(R\)-algebra.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.2.7. Opposite algebra.

    Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, and let \(A\) be a right \(R\)-algebra. We denote by \(A^{op}\) the opposite ring of \(A\text{.}\) Recall that \(A^{op}\) as a set is equal to \(A\text{.}\) Furthemore, it satisfies the following relations for any \(a^{op},b^{op}\in A^{op}\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} a^{op}+b^{op}=(a+b)^{op},\quad\text{and}\quad a^{op}b^{op}=(ba)^{op} \end{equation*}
    Then \(A^{op}\) is a right \(R\)-algebra, called the opposite algebra of \(A\text{,}\) gievn by the scalar multiplication
    \begin{equation*} a^{op}\cdot r=(a\cdot r)^{op} \quad\text{for any }r\in R\text{ and }a^{op}\in A^{op}. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    We discuss the example of ‘Group algebra’ in the next section.
