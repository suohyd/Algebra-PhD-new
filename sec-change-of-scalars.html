    Section 3.1 Change of scalars

    Throughout this section we assume that \(R\) is a commutative ring with unity, and that \(A\) is a nonzero unital right \(R\)-algebra. All \(R\)-algebras will be right \(R\)-algebras unless otherwise stated.
    🔗

    Definition 3.1.1.

    Suppose that \(0\neq A\) and \(B\) are right \(R\)-algebras. Let \(\theta\colon A\to B\) be an \(R\)-algebra homomorphism. Let \(M\) be a right \(B\)-module. We can make \(M\) a right \(A\)-module by defining
    \begin{equation*} m\cdot a := m\cdot \theta(a)\quad\text{for all } m\in M \text{ and } a\in A. \end{equation*}
    We denote by \(M_A\) the right \(A\)-module \(M\) with this action. The process of obtaining the right \(A\)-module \(M_A\) from the right \(B\)-module \(M\) is called change of scalars from \(B\) to \(A\) via the homomorphism \(\theta\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Lemma 3.1.2.

    Let \(\theta\colon A\to B\) be an \(R\)-algebra homomorphism, and let \(M\) and \(N\) be right \(B\)-modules.
    1. Direct sum and right \(A\)-module action commutes with each other:
      \begin{equation*} \left(M\oplus N\right)_A \simeq M_A \oplus N_A \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
    2. If \(f\in\Hom{B}{M}{N}\) then, \(f\in\Hom{A}{M_A}{N_A}\text{.}\) Furthermore, if \(\theta\) is surjective then, \(\Hom{B}{M}{N}=\Hom{A}{M_A}{N_A}\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. If \(N\leq M\) then, \(N_A\leq M_A\text{.}\) If \(\theta\) is surjective then, the sets \(S(M)\) and \(S(M_A)\) of the submodules of \(M\) and \(M_A\) respectively, are equal.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 3.1.3. Annihilator of a module and faithful module.

    Let \(A\) be a nonzero \(R\)-algebra and \(M\) be a right \(A\)-module. Let \(X\subseteq M\text{.}\) The annihilator of \(X\) in \(A\) is defined as
    \begin{equation*} \Ann{A}{X} = \{a\in A: x\cdot a = 0 \text{ for all } x\in X\}. \end{equation*}
    If \(\Ann{A}{M}=\{0\}\) then, we say that \(M\) is a faithful right \(A\)-module.
    🔗
    If \(X=\{x\}\subseteq M\) then, we write \(\Ann{A}{x}\) instead of \(\Ann{A}{\{x\}}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Lemma 3.1.4.

    Let \(M,M'\text{,}\) and \(\{M_j:j\in J\}\) be right \(A\)-modules. Suppose that \(X\subseteq M\) and \(Y\subseteq M\text{.}\) We have the following:
    1. \(\Ann{A}{X}\) is a right ideal of \(A\text{.}\) If \(X\) is a submodule of \(M\) then, \(\Ann{A}{X}\) is a two-sided ideal of \(A\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. If \(X\subseteq Y\) then, \(\Ann{A}{Y}\subseteq \Ann{A}{X}\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. If \(M\simeq M'\) then, \(\Ann{A}{M}=\Ann{A}{M'}\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    4. If \(M=\sum_{j\in J}M_j\) then, \(\Ann{A}{M}=\cap_{j\in J}\Ann{A}{M_j}\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    5. If \(M\) is a right ideal of \(A\) then, \(\Ann{A}{A/M}\) is the largest two-sided ideal of \(A\) contained in \(M\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.1.5. Minimal polynomial of a linear transformation as a generator of an annihilator.

    Let \(V\) be a finite-dimensional vector space over a field \(F\text{,}\) and \(T\colon V\to V\) be an \(F\)-linear transformation. Let \(A=F[x]\) be the polynomial ring in one variable over \(F\text{.}\) We can make \(V\) a right \(A\)-module by defining
    \begin{equation*} v\cdot f(x) := f(T)(v) \quad\text{for all } v\in V \text{ and } f(x)\in A. \end{equation*}
    The annihilator of \(V\) in \(A\) is a nonzero ideal of \(A\) because \(V\) is finite-dimensional. As \(A\) is a principal ideal domain, there exists a unique monic polynomial \(p(x)\in A\) such that \(\Ann{A}{V} =(p(x))\text{.}\) The polynomial \(p(x)\) is called the minimal polynomial of the linear transformation \(T\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.1.6. Modules over simple algebras are faithful.

    Let \(A\) be a finite-dimension simple \(R\)-algebra, see Definition 2.1.6. If \(M\) is a nonzero right \(A\)-module then, \(M\) is a faithful right \(A\)-module. Indeed, by Lemma 3.1.4, \(\Ann{A}{M}\) is a two-sided ideal of \(A\text{.}\) As \(A\) is simple, either \(\Ann{A}{M}=A\) or \(\Ann{A}{M}=0\text{.}\) If \(\Ann{A}{M}=A\) then, \(m\cdot 1=0\) for every \(m\in M\text{.}\) This implies that \(M=\{0\}\text{,}\) which is a contradiction. Hence, \(\Ann{A}{M}=0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.1.7.

    Let \(M\) be a right \(A\)-module, and let \(x\in M\) be a nonzero element. Consider \(xA\) a submodule of \(M\) generated by \(x\text{.}\) The map \(\varphi\colon A\to xA\) defined by \(\varphi(a)=x\cdot a\) is a surjective homomorphism of right \(A\)-modules. By the first isomorphism theorem, we have that \(xA\simeq A/\ker\varphi\text{.}\) As \(\ker\varphi=\Ann{A}{x}\text{,}\) we conclude that
    \begin{equation*} xA\simeq A/\Ann{A}{x}. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    Proposition 3.1.8.

    Let \(A,B\) be \(R\)-algebras, and \(\theta\colon A\to B\) a surjective \(R\)-algebra homomorphism. If \(N\) is a right \(A\)-module then, there exists a right \(B\)-module \(M\) such that \(N\simeq M_A\) if and only if \(\ker\theta\subseteq\Ann{A}{N}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Corollary 3.1.9.

    Let \(I\) be a two-sided ideal of an \(R\)-algebra \(A\text{,}\) and \(N\) is a right \(A/I\)-module. The module \(N\) is a faithful right \(A/I\)-module if and only if \(\Ann{A}{N_A}=I\text{.}\)
