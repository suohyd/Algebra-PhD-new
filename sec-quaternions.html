    Section 2.1 Quaternion algebra

    Throughout this section we assume that \(F\) is a field with \({\rm ch}(F)\neq 2\text{.}\) The set of nonzero elements of \(F\) is denoted by \(\dot{F}\text{.}\)

    Definition 2.1.1. Quaternion algebra.

    Let \(a,b\in\dot{F}\text{.}\) The quaternion algebra \((a,b)_F\) or \(\left(\tfrac{a,b}{F}\right)\) is the four-dimensional \(F\)-vector space with basis \(\{1,i,j,k\}\) and multiplication defined by the following relations and with \(1\) as unity of \((a,b)_F\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation} i^2 = a\cdot 1, \quad j^2 = b\cdot 1, \quad ij = k = -ji.\tag{2.1.1} \end{equation}
    We assume the associativity of multiplication and extend the multiplication to all elements of \((a,b)_F\) by distributivity and linearity.

    \begin{equation} k^2 = -ab\cdot 1, \quad jk = -kj = -bi, \quad ki = -ik = -aj.\tag{2.1.2} \end{equation}

    Definition 2.1.6. Simple algebra.

    Let \(A\) be an \(F\)-algebra of finite dimension as an \(F\)-vector space. We say that \(A\) is a simple algebra if the only two-sided ideals of \(A\) are \(0\) and \(A\text{.}\)

    Definition 2.1.8. Canonical involution of Quaternion algebra.

    Let \(F\) be a field of characteristic not \(2\text{,}\) and \((a,b)_F\) a quaternion algebra. The canonical involution on \((a,b)_F\) is \(\gamma\colon(a,b)_F\to(a,b)_F\) given by
    \begin{equation*} a_0+a_1i+a_2j+a_3k\mapsto a_0-a_1i-a_2j-a_3k. \end{equation*}
