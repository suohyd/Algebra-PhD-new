    Section 3.4 Semisimple modules

    In this section we assume that \(R\) is a commutative ring with unity and \(A\) is a right \(R\)-algebra. For a right \(A\)-module \(M\) we denote by \(S(M)\) the collection of all \(A\)-submodules of \(M\text{.}\)

    Definition 3.4.1. Complement of a submodule.

    Assume that \(R\) is a commutative ring with unity and \(A\) is a right \(R\)-algebra. Let \(M\) be a right \(A\)-module and \(P,N\leq M\) be \(A\)-submodules of \(M\text{.}\) The module \(P\) is called a complement of \(N\) in \(S(M)\) if \(M=P\oplus N\text{.}\)

    Lemma 3.4.2.

    Let \(M=\sum_{j\in J}N_j\text{,}\) where \(N_j\) is a simple \(A\)-submodule of \(M\text{.}\) If \(P\leq M\) then, there exists \(I\subseteq J\) such that
    \begin{equation*} M=\left(\bigoplus_{i\in I}N_i\right)\bigoplus P. \end{equation*}

    Proof.

    Consider the following set. \(\Sigma=\left\{\{N_i\}_{i\in I}\cup\{P\}:\sum_{i\in I}N_i+P=\left(\bigoplus_{i\in I} N_i\right)\bigoplus P\text{ for some subset }I\subseteq J\right\}\) The set \(\Sigma\neq\emptyset\) as empty sum of submodules is considered as \(\{0\}\text{.}\) Suppose that we have a chain of submodules corresponding to a chain of subsets \(\{I_{k}\}\) of \(J\text{.}\) Then, put \(I=\cup I_k\text{.}\) We claim that
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{i\in I}N_i+P=\left(\bigoplus_{i\in I}N_i\right)\bigoplus P. \end{equation*}
    For any
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{r=1}^sn_{i_r}+p\in \sum_{t\in I_k}N_t+P=\left(\bigoplus_{t\in I_k}N_t\right)\bigoplus P\text{ for some }k. \end{equation*}
    This shows that every chain has an upper bound. By Zornâ€™s lemma there exists a maximal element, say a subset \(I\subseteq J\) such that \(\sum_{i\in I}N_i+P=\left(\bigoplus N_i\right)\bigoplus P\text{.}\) Put \(M_1=\sum_{i\in I}N_i+P\text{.}\) By maximality of \(I\text{,}\) \(M_1\cap N_j\neq 0\) for every \(j\in J\text{.}\) As \(N_j\) is simple, we must have \(M_1\cap N_j=N_j\text{,}\) i.e., \(N_j\subseteq M_1\) for each \(j\in J\text{.}\) Therefore, we get the result.
